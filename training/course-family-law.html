Module 1: Understanding Marriage Regimes in South Africa

Family law governs the relationships, rights, and duties within family structures. One of the first and most critical decisions a couple makes is their marital regime. This choice has significant financial consequences, especially during a divorce. In South Africa, there are three main types.

A. Types of Marriages

  • Civil Marriage: This is a monogamous marriage between a man and a woman, governed by the Marriage Act.
  • Civil Union: This allows same-sex or opposite-sex couples to enter into a legal union with the same consequences as a civil marriage. Governed by the Civil Union Act.
  • Customary Marriage: A marriage negotiated, celebrated or concluded according to any of the systems of indigenous African customary law that exist in South Africa. This is recognised by the Recognition of Customary Marriages Act. Importantly, if a man is in a customary marriage, he cannot enter into a civil marriage with another woman, but he can enter into further customary marriages.

B. The Financial Consequences: Your Marital Regime

When you get married, you must decide how your assets will be treated. If you do not sign an Antenuptial Contract (ANC) before getting married, you are automatically married In Community of Property.
