Co-Parenting 101

Master the art of raising a child with your co-parent, even after separation.

Module 1: The Foundation of Effective Co-Parenting

Co-parenting is not about your relationship with your ex-partner; it is about your shared relationship with your child. It requires shifting your focus from being romantic partners to being parenting partners. The goal is to provide a stable, loving, and consistent environment for your child, free from the conflict of the adult relationship.

A. What is Co-Parenting?

It's an agreement where both parents take an active role in raising their child, despite not being in a relationship. Good co-parenting is crucial for a child's emotional and psychological well-being. Research consistently shows that children who have positive relationships with both parents after a separation have better outcomes in life.

B. The Golden Rule: Child's Best Interests

Every decision you make as co-parents must be guided by one question: What is in the best interests of our child? This aligns directly with Section 28 of the Constitution. It means putting aside personal feelings, ego, and past grievances to focus solely on what will help your child thrive. This is your most powerful guiding principle.
