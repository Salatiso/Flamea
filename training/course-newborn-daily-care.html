Module 1: The First Days - Your Role as a Father

Becoming a father is a profound transformation. The first few days and weeks with your newborn are crucial for bonding and establishing your role as an active, capable parent. This module will equip you with the foundational knowledge to navigate this special time with confidence.

A. Your Greatest Asset: The Extended Family

In South Africa, our greatest strength is often our family. Many men live in multi-generational homes with their mothers, sisters, and cousins. This is not a weakness; it is your ultimate support system. When you go to work, the child's grandmother (Gogo/Makhulu), who raised you, is there. Your sister, who knows your family's ways, is there. This is a powerful advantage. Mothers have relied on this system for generations. It is time for fathers to recognise and proudly leverage this same strength.

  • Global Practice (30%): Around the world, communities have always relied on grandparents and extended family for childcare.
  • SA Context (70%): In isiXhosa culture, the concept of `ikhaya` (the home) extends beyond just parents. In Zulu culture, `umndeni` (family) is central. Lean on this wisdom. Ask your mother for her advice, allow your sister to show you how she swaddles her baby. This collaboration is your strength.
