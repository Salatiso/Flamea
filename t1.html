Step 1: Child & Parent Details

South African Constitution (Section 28) and Children’s Act (Section 18) prioritize the child’s best interests, with equal parental rights by default (UNCRC Article 18).

Step 2: Relationship & Agreements

Children’s Act (Section 33) encourages parental agreements. Equality Act ensures fairness (UNCRC Article 3).

Step 3: Custody Arrangements

Children’s Act (Section 18(2)) defines custody types. Primary residence is critical (UNCRC Article 9).

Step 4: Visitation Schedules

Children’s Act (Section 7) emphasizes the child’s best interests in scheduling (UNCRC Article 12).

Step 5: Communication & Decision-Making

Children’s Act (Section 18(2)(b)) supports co-parenting communication (UNCRC Article 5).

Step 6: Financial Responsibilities

Maintenance Act governs support; Equality Act ensures fairness (UNCRC Article 27).

Step 7: Dispute Resolution

Children’s Act (Section 34) and Family Advocate support mediation (UNCRC Article 12).
